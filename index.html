About Me

Born and raised in northern Mexico in the later 80's, I developed an early fascination with how things are made and how they work. This curiosity led me on an exploratory journey, from nature to art and architecture throughout my childhood. As a teenager, I discovered the world of music and digital media, creating projects in video, illustration, and graphic design.

In college, I dabbled in various art workshops (which still pl ay an important role in my craftsy side) and gained introductory knowledge in coding. To my surprise, I actually enjoyed it—but at the time, I was set on becoming an artist, so I chose to ignore it.

That changed years later while working at IBM, where I had the opportunity to build a news portal on a local server. The process of structuring and developing it reignited my enthusiasm for coding. It felt like uncovering the mechanics of a complex system, much like my curiosity for understanding how things work. At the same time, it resonated with my passion for project management—the need to have a clear scope of how processes are structured and optimized.

After graduating, I spent ten years working professionally in graphic design, balancing both employment and freelance opportunities. Yet, coding remained in the back of my mind. I finally decided to dive in, starting with HTML—and I loved it. From there, I kept pushing forward: first with basic code structures, then Bootstrap, then WordPress. Eventually, I wanted to customize things beyond what WordPress allowed, so I aimed higher and started researching. That's when I discovered W3Schools and freeCodeCamp, marking the beginning of my current learning journey.

Now, my goal is to develop applications that contribute to solving global resource management and distribution challenges while also helping the environment. Just like when I first started exploring how things work, I want to use my skills to create solutions that improve the way the world functions.
